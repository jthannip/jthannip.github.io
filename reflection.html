Professional growth

Critical Analysis.

My journey through the ENGR 499 Capstone course has fundamentally reshaped my understanding of the design process. I learned that engineering is not a linear sequence of calculations but a cycle of constant iteration. Our team’s decision to move from a rooftop to a ground-mounted system taught me that a "perfect" technical solution is useless if it ignores real-world structural constraints.


I believe the most important attributes of an engineer are resilience and social responsibility. This project showed me that the role of a professional engineer is to protect public welfare. Whether it was implementing 48V DC architecture for safety or choosing non-toxic LiFePO4 batteries to protect the environment, every technical decision carries an ethical weight. Our work wasn't just about kilowatts; it was about providing safety and dignity to families in Cuba.


Reflecting on my time at the UBCO School of Engineering, my greatest strength is my ability to communicate complex engineering logic through physical prototyping. However, I recognize a need for growth in managing large-scale project documentation. I plan to facilitate this by pursuing a Junior Engineer role that offers mentorship in project management while I work toward my P.Eng designation.
